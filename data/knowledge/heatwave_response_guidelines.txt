# Heatwave Response Guidelines

## Objective
- Reduce heat-related illness and maintain continuity of critical city services.

## Activation Triggers
- Forecast indicates sustained high temperature and high nighttime minimums.
- Heat index exceeds local health-risk threshold.
- Health services report rising heat-stress incidents.

## Immediate Operations (0–2 Hours)
- Activate heat response coordination and assign lead departments.
- Extend cooling-center hours and verify staffing.
- Prioritize welfare checks for elderly and medically vulnerable residents.
- Coordinate hydration support at high-footfall transit locations.

## Stabilization Actions (2–24 Hours)
- Increase frequency of public transport and public-space heat monitoring.
- Shift non-critical outdoor municipal work to cooler hours.
- Ensure backup power readiness for health and cooling facilities.
- Review district-level demand for water and shade resources.

## Public Safety Messaging
- Issue practical guidance: hydration, shade, reduced exertion, symptom awareness.
- Publish cooling-center locations and operating hours.
- Repeat advisories at fixed intervals during peak heat windows.

## Stand-Down Criteria
- Heat index returns to non-critical range for sustained period.
- Emergency service heat-related call volume normalizes.
- Cooling-center surge demand subsides.
