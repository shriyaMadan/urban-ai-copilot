# Public Communication Templates

## Objective
- Deliver clear, consistent, and actionable public messages during city risk events.

## Core Message Structure
- Situation: what is happening now.
- Impact: who or what is affected.
- Action: what residents should do immediately.
- Timing: when next update will be issued.

## Template: Initial Advisory
- "City operations advisory: a risk event is active in the affected district."
- "Expected impact: localized disruption to mobility, services, or public safety conditions."
- "Please follow the top protective actions issued by the city response team."
- "The next city update will be issued through official city channels."

## Template: Escalation Notice
- "Update: risk level has increased in the affected district."
- "Avoid affected roads, exposed zones, and non-essential movement."
- "If affected: follow immediate safety instructions and use the designated city contact channel."
- "Emergency access routes are being prioritized."

## Template: Stabilization Update
- "Update: conditions are stabilizing in the affected district."
- "Some temporary restrictions remain in selected locations."
- "Continue following guidance until full all-clear is issued."

## Template: All-Clear Notice
- "All-clear: conditions have returned to operationally safe levels."
- "Residual caution may remain in isolated streets, drainage points, or transport corridors."
- "City services returning to standard schedule."

## Quality Controls
- Use plain language and avoid technical jargon.
- Include timestamp and geographic scope in every message.
- Keep message length short; prioritize actionable instructions.
