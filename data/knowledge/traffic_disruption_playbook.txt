# Traffic Disruption Playbook

## Objective
- Maintain emergency access, reduce network gridlock, and protect commuter safety.

## Activation Triggers
- Congestion index exceeds critical threshold on primary corridors.
- Multi-node incidents affect arterial routes or public transport hubs.
- Weather impacts are likely to cause sustained speed reductions.

## Immediate Operations (0–2 Hours)
- Activate traffic operations lead and incident command channel.
- Prioritize clearance on emergency routes and hospital access roads.
- Deploy dynamic signal timing for affected corridors.
- Publish immediate rerouting instructions for major bottlenecks.

## Stabilization Actions (2–12 Hours)
- Coordinate lane management and temporary diversions.
- Increase incident-response patrol presence at recurrent choke points.
- Align transit operators on frequency adjustments and passenger messaging.
- Reassess corridor priorities each operations cycle.

## Public Guidance
- Provide route alternatives with expected delay ranges.
- Recommend staggered travel times for non-essential trips.
- Communicate transit substitutions where available.

## Stand-Down Criteria
- Critical corridors return to acceptable flow conditions.
- Incident queue length declines below operational threshold.
- Emergency and public transport reliability is restored.
