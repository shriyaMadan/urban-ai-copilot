# Flood Response Guidelines

## Objective
- Protect life, critical infrastructure, and mobility corridors during flood risk periods.

## Activation Triggers
- Rainfall forecast exceeds local drainage capacity thresholds.
- River level trend is rising rapidly.
- Field reports confirm recurring waterlogging in vulnerable districts.

## Immediate Operations (0–2 Hours)
- Activate city coordination cell and assign incident lead.
- Prioritize drainage inspection at known hotspot junctions.
- Pre-stage pumps, barriers, and field crews in low-lying zones.
- Coordinate with transport teams on underpass and tunnel monitoring.

## Stabilization Actions (2–12 Hours)
- Reassess district risk every operational cycle.
- Restrict access to repeatedly flooded streets.
- Protect critical facilities (hospitals, power, transit nodes).
- Maintain resource reserve for secondary incidents.

## Public Safety Messaging
- Issue short district-specific advisories with clear actions.
- Publish safe-route and avoidance guidance for commuters.
- Repeat updates at fixed intervals with timestamped status.

## Stand-Down Criteria
- Rainfall intensity and river trend return to stable range.
- Critical routes are passable and monitored.
- No active life-safety incidents remain.
